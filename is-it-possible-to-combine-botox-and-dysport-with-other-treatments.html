Is it possible to combine Botox and Dysport with other treatments?

Patients often ask for information about additional treatments, such as Botox and Dysport. You can also get injectable fillers that can be used to plump your skin or add volume. Juvederm and Restylane contain hyaluronic acids, which is a naturally occurring compound in skin. A patient may receive a liquid facelift when Botox and Dysport are combined. Liquid facelifts are becoming a widely popular option since you can avoid major surgery altogether.

Patients may also inquire about alternative treatments such as chemical peels or hydrafacials. It's understandable that these patients would want the best treatment as quickly as possible. However, each procedure happens on a case-by-case basis. Your dermatologist will err on the side of caution, making sure to complete your Botox or Dysport treatments first. Once all goes well, you can come back for another treatment to treat another problem area at another time.

Botox results

You may start seeing results from Botox within a week, but full results can take up to 1 month. Botox injections can last for several months, some even lasting up to 6 months.

Botox and Dysport are for adults 18 years or older with mild to moderate facial lines. To determine whether you are a suitable candidate, your doctor will examine your medical history.

You may not be eligible for any of these procedures if:

  • are pregnant
  • Have a history botulinum-toxin sensitivity
  • have a milk allergy
  • Have a skin condition
  • Your doctor will determine if you have thicker skin
  • Are over 65
  • Take certain medicines

Many medications can interact with injection ingredients. These include blood thinners and muscle relaxers.

You should be aware that injections can interact with anticholinergic medications used to treat Parkinson's disease.

Before you stop taking any medication, consult your doctor. You should also inform your doctor about any medications or supplements that you use, regardless of whether they are prescriptions.
