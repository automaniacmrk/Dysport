What can you expect during your Dysport appointment?

A Dysport procedure is short and sweet. The physician will first clean the area to be treated and then determine where the drug should be injected. The actual injection will take place after that. The whole process takes only a few minutes unless some form of pain medication is used.

The FDA approves Dysport cosmetic injections for the treatment of blemishes referred to as "frown lines" or "elevens." These are wrinkles found in the glabellar area--the region between your eyebrows. However, an experienced dermatologist may use it off-label, or without FDA approval, for other skin issues like:

  • The wrinkles near your eyes are called "Crows feet", or simply "Crows Feet".
  • Fine lines around the lips
  • Forehead lines
  • Excessive sweating
  • Acne
  • Some forms of chronic dermatitis

And many others.

Relaxed Muscles

Botulinum Toxin A is only given in small amounts so that its effects can be severely weakened. This doesn't impact the efficacy of treatment but rather ensures a safe therapy for you. The muscle stops receiving messages and relaxes. This makes wrinkles disappear.

As a short-term treatment, this neurotoxin helps your face look softer, smoother, and more youthful. But in the long-term, it prevents lines and wrinkles from forming more deeply. We are essentially saying that lines won't form if you reduce muscle activity. The treatment preserves the integrity of your skin and continues to do so until you get injections.

What Are The Complications Associated With Dysport(r)?

These complications are very rare. They include paralysis of the nearby muscle resulting in temporary loss of function i.e., drooping eyelid, in approximately 2% of injections. Sometimes, temporary headaches and bruising have been reported.

In rare cases, the effect of botulinum toxin may affect areas of the body away from the injection site and cause symptoms of a serious condition called botulism. The symptoms of botulism include: loss of strength and muscle weakness all over the body, double vision, blurred vision and drooping eyelids, hoarseness or change or loss of voice (dysphonia), trouble saying words clearly (dysarthria), loss of bladder control, trouble breathing, trouble swallowing

In a certain number of individuals the injection does not work as satisfactorily or for as long as usual.

Dysport Aftercare Is Quick And Painless

Aftercare for Dysport is the same as that needed by Botox.

  • Some activity restriction is required in the first few hours after injection to prevent spreading the neurotoxin to distant sites.
  • Protection of the treated area from infection, trauma and the sun is crucial in the next few days.
  • Follow the instructions of your dermatologist for post-treatment care.
  • There are many options for treating post-injection bruises.
  • To maintain the effects of the drug long term, healthy lifestyle habits and prompt repeat injections are important.

What is the Most Vital Information About Dysport I Need to Know?

Some people with breathing difficulties may require the use of muscles in their necks to aid them. These patients may be at greater risk for serious breathing problems with Dysport.

Swallowing difficulties can last several weeks. A feeding tube may be necessary for people who are unable to swallow. Food and liquids can get into your lungs if you have severe swallowing difficulties. People who already have swallowing or breathing problems before receiving Dysport are at higher risk for these problems.

Botulinum canxin injections may cause botulism symptoms in some instances.

  • Loss of strength and muscle weakness all over the body
  • Blurred and double-vision
  • Eyelids that are drooping
  • Dysphonia is characterized by hoarseness, change in voice or loss (hysphonia).
  • Trouble saying words clearly (dysarthria)
  • Loss of bladder control
  • Trouble breathing and swallowing.

These symptoms can happen anywhere from hours to weeks after you receive an injection of Dysport.

Botox results

Botox may begin to show results within one week. However, full results could take as long as a month. Botox injections also last a few months at a time, with some lasting upward of 6 months.

Botox and Dysport are for adults 18 years or older with mild to moderate facial lines. Your doctor will review your medical history and ask you some questions to determine if you're a good candidate.

You may not be eligible for any of these procedures if:

  • are pregnant
  • have a history of botulinum toxin sensitivity
  • Have a milk allergy
  • Have a skin condition
  • have thick skin (as determined by your doctor)
  • are over 65 years old
  • take certain medications

Several medications may interact with the ingredients in the injections. This includes blood thinners as well as muscle relaxers.

The injections may also interact with certain medications that affect your muscles, such as anticholinergics used for Parkinson's disease.

Before you stop taking any medication, consult your doctor. You should also inform your doctor about any medications or supplements that you use, regardless of whether they are prescriptions.
